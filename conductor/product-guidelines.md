# Product Guidelines: Maternity Pay Calculator

## Tone and Voice
- **Efficient & Direct**: Communication should be concise, direct, and data-focused. Users are looking for specific answers and should be able to find them without unnecessary fluff.
- **Clarity**: Ensure financial terms and calculations are explained simply and clearly.

## Visual Branding
- **Soft & Calming**: Use a palette of calming blues, soft teals, and clean whites to create a stress-free environment for users planning their leave.
- **Typography**: Clean, readable sans-serif fonts should be used throughout.

## UX Principles
- **Simplicity & Clarity**: Prioritize clean layouts with plenty of whitespace and a clear visual hierarchy. The user should never feel overwhelmed by data.
- **Instant Feedback**: Inputs must be validated immediately. Any changes to salary or policy should reflect in the calculations in real-time.
- **Data Visualization (Graphs)**: Graphs are a core component. Use visual representations (e.g., bar charts, area charts) to show the payment timeline clearly.
- **Accessibility**: Ensure high contrast and mobile-first design for ease of use across all devices.

## Interactive Feedback
- **Subtle Animations**: Use subtle micro-interactions and transitions to provide feedback on state changes (e.g., when updating a month, the graph should animate smoothly).
- **Clear Indicators**: Use distinct visual cues to indicate valid inputs or errors.
